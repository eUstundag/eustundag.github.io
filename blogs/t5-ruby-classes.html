We have now moved onto classes and slightly more complex data structures. We are set to tackle some more difficult challenges, requiring a snowballing effort of our previously gained knowledge. We were given tasks to work on nested data structures, tricky algorithms, and a bingo game to create.

This week has helped me a lot with just practicing on restructuring using classes and attributes. Take for example, we want to implement a warrior class.

  • class Warrior
  • attr_accessor :sword,:armor,:level
  • def initialize(sword = nil, armor = nil, level = nil)
  • @sword = sword
  • @armor = armor
  • @level = nil
  • end
  • end

This class, can be accessed with: Arthur = Warrior.new. This will call upon our warrior, Arthur, who has no sword, no armor, and is a complete newbie. We can also assign him some gear, and assume that he has had some training and modify his level. Arthur.sword = "Excalibur". Arthur.armor = "Green Armour". And, we can't send Arthur out into the world without some training, so Arthur.level = 5.

puts "Arthur was about to retrieve his #{Arthur.sword} and with his #{Arthur.armor}, he felt ready for battle. It did not take long for Arthur to surpass his level #{#Arthur.level} status in the world."

=> Arthur was about to retrieve his Excalibur and with his Green Armour, he felt ready for battle. It did not take long for Arthur to surpass his level 5 status in the world.

We can do some other cool stuff such as adding a method to the class.

  • def sword_attack
  • rand(100)
  • end

We summon Arthur's amazing sword play abilities to defend our lands and we do this by: Arthur.sword_attack. This will give us how much damage he inflicts every time sword_attack is called.
